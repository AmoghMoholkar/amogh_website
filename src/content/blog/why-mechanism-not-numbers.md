Almost every marketing datasheet for a carbon-fiber-reinforced filament leads with the same two numbers: higher tensile strength, higher stiffness. Those numbers are true and also not that interesting on their own, because they don't tell you anything about **why** the part is stronger, or what happens on the other side of yield.

The question I actually care about is mechanistic: does adding short carbon fiber to PETG change the **deformation and failure mechanism** — where strain localizes, how a crack initiates and propagates, whether failure becomes more brittle or stays ductile — or does it just scale the same underlying PETG behavior up?

That's the difference between a datasheet number and something a designer can actually use to decide *where* to place a carbon-fiber part in a load path, and it's the question the full factorial DIC and fractography study currently in progress is built around.
