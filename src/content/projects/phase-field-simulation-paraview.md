Phase-field simulations produce a lot of numerical output that's hard to reason about until it's visualized properly. This project focused on the **post-processing and visualization side** — taking raw phase-field simulation output and rendering microstructure evolution (grain growth, phase boundary migration) in **ParaView** in a way that actually communicates what's physically happening, rather than just producing a technically correct but unreadable plot.

It's a smaller project, but it directly informs how I think about presenting microstructural data now — including the DIC and fractography figures in later, larger projects.
