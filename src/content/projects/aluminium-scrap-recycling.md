Recycled aluminium is attractive on cost and sustainability grounds, but scrap streams are rarely well-characterized before remelting, which shows up later as inconsistent mechanical properties. This project examined how **scrap composition and processing parameters** during recovery affect the resulting alloy's microstructure and mechanical behavior.

The practical takeaway: a meaningful fraction of the "recycled material is inferior" narrative is really a **process-control problem**, not an inherent property of recycled feedstock.
